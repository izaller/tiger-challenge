This past semester, I worked with three other Princeton students and Fair Share Housing Center of NJ to build an affordable housing search tool. I really enjoyed this work, as it allowed me to combine my love of computer science and my desire to help others. After completing this very fulfilling project, I was curious to see if there was more work I could be doing to help other nonprofits.

Then, it hit me. The light bulb turned on. There must be other qualified CS students, both at Princeton and elsewhere, who felt the same way. And what's a better use of my time: to help one nonprofit at a time? or to build a platform which could connect nonprofits in need of technical assistance (building websites, running analyses, etc.) to CS students with some time on their hands and a desire in their hearts to make change?

And thus, this website was created. What you see now is an extremely bare-bones mock-up I made for this platform.

I would be absolutely thrilled to apply my maker mindset to a Tiger Challenge this summer.
